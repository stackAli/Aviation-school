What Is the Recreational Pilot Licence (RPL)?

The RPL is the perfect entry point into aviation for new pilots in Australia. Issued by the Civil Aviation Safety Authority (CASA), this licence enables you to fly light, single-engine aircraft as pilot-in-command—without supervision—within 25 nautical miles of your departure aerodrome.

It’s ideal for personal use, hobby flying, and those beginning the pathway to more advanced licences such as PPL or CPL.

The RPL gives you the essential skills and foundation to fly safely and confidently in real-world conditions.

How Do You Qualify for the RPL?

  • Be at least 16 years old
  • Log a minimum of 25 flight hours (20 dual + 5 solo)
  • Pass the RPL theory exam and a CASA flight test
  • Hold a valid aviation medical certificate
  • Demonstrate English language proficiency

At Goldwing’s Aviation (Moorabbin Airport), your RPL includes endorsements for controlled airspace operations, radio communication, and aerodrome procedures—preparing you for confident flying from day one.
