Recreational Pilot Licence (RPL)-

Your First Step into the Skies

The Recreational Pilot Licence (RPL) is the ideal entry point for aspiring pilots in Australia. Issued by the Civil Aviation Safety Authority (CASA), the RPL allows you to fly light, single-engine aircraft as pilot in command—without supervision—within 25 nautical miles of your departure aerodrome.

Whether you're flying for fun or building toward a career, the RPL is your launchpad into aviation.

To qualify, you must:

  • Be at least 16 years old
  • Log a minimum of 25 flight hours (including 20 dual and 5 solo)
  • Pass the RPL theory exam and a CASA flight test
  • Hold a valid medical certificate
  • Demonstrate English language proficiency

The Recreational Pilots License at Moorabbin Airport includes endorsements for controlled airspace and aerodrome operations, and radio use.
