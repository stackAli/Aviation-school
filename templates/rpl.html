Recreational Pilot Licence (RPL) – Your First Step into the Skies

The RPL is the ideal entry point for aspiring pilots in Australia. Issued by the Civil Aviation Safety Authority (CASA), it allows you to fly light, single-engine aircraft as pilot in command—without supervision—within 25 nautical miles of your departure aerodrome.

Whether you're flying for fun or building toward a career, the RPL is your launchpad into aviation.

How Do You Qualify for the RPL?

  • Be at least 16 years old
  • Complete a minimum of 25 flight hours (including 20 dual and 5 solo)
  • Pass a theory exam and a flight test
  • Hold a valid medical certificate
  • Demonstrate English language proficiency

The RPL at Moorabbin Airport includes endorsements for controlled airspace and aerodrome operations, and radio use—setting you up for broader flying privileges.
